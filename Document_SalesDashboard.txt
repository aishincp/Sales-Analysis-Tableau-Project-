Sales dashboard Purpose:
The purpose of sales dashboard is to present an overview of the sakes metrixs & trends in order to analyze year-over-year sales performance & understand sales trends.

Introduction:
This user story outlines the specifications for building two dashboards using Tableau to help stakeholders, including sales managers, and executives to analyze sales performance & customers.

Key requirements:
KPI Overview:
Display a summary of total sales, profits, and quantity for the current year & the previous year.

Sales Trends:
- Present the data each for KPI on a monthly basis for both current & previous year.
- Identify months with  highest & lowest sales and make them easy to recognize

Product Subcategory Comparison:
- Compare sales performance by different product subcategories for current & previous year
- Include a comparison of sales with profit.

Weekly Trends for Sales & Profit:
- Present weekly sales & profit data for current year
- Display the avg weekly values.
- Highlights weeks that are above & below the avg to draw attention to sales & profit performance.

Monthly Trends:
- Identify months with highest & lowest sales & make them easy to recognize

Customer Distribution by Number of Orders:
- Represent the distribution of customers based on the no. of orders they have placed to provide insights into customer insights.

Top 10 Customers By Profit:
- Present the top 10 customers who have generated the highest profits for the company.
- Show additional informations like rank, number of orders, current sales, current profit & last order date.

## DESIGN & INTERACTIVITY REQUIREMENTS
Dashboard Dynamic:
- The Dashboard should allow users to check historical data by offering them the flexibility to select any desired year. for ex. 2021, 2022 or so on
- Provide users with the ability to navigate between dashboards easily. Smooth transitions
- Make charts & graphs interactive, enabling users to filter data using the charts

Data Filters:
- Allow users to filter data by product information like category / subcategory and by location information like region, state & city

KPI Overview -> KPI cards

Current Year -> Line Chart
Previous Year -> Sparkline

Product Category/Subcategory -> Bar Charts (Bar-in-Bar to compare for both years)
Sales with Profit -> Bar Chart

Weekly / Monthly Sales & Profit ( which means Changes Over Time) -> Line Chart
